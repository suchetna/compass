# Governance & Risk Matrix

A starting framework for defining risk tiers and human-in-the-loop requirements by workflow type. Adapt the tiers and examples to your organization's actual risk profile — this is a starting scaffold, not a universal standard.

## Risk tiers

### Tier 1 — Low risk
**Examples:** internal FAQ drafts, social media copy variants, routine status updates, meeting summaries
**AI autonomy:** AI can draft and publish with spot-check review, not line-by-line approval
**Human checkpoint:** Periodic quality audit (e.g., weekly sample review), not per-item sign-off
**Accountability:** Team lead, reviewed in aggregate

### Tier 2 — Medium risk
**Examples:** external blog content, employer branding materials, non-crisis media pitches, internal leadership communications
**AI autonomy:** AI drafts; a named human reviews and edits before publishing
**Human checkpoint:** Per-item review required before publication
**Accountability:** Content owner, named per piece

### Tier 3 — High risk
**Examples:** press releases, investor communications, anything with legal or regulatory exposure, executive public statements
**AI autonomy:** AI can assist with research, drafting structure, or first-pass language — but final language requires full human authorship or heavy human rewrite
**Human checkpoint:** Multi-stage review (comms lead + legal/compliance where relevant) before publication
**Accountability:** Senior comms leader, documented sign-off

### Tier 4 — Critical
**Examples:** crisis statements, regulatory disclosures, anything involving safety, litigation, or significant reputational exposure
**AI autonomy:** AI assists with research and background only — no AI-generated language ships without full rewrite and senior sign-off
**Human checkpoint:** Executive-level review, likely with legal involvement
**Accountability:** CCO or equivalent, explicitly documented

---

## Using this matrix

1. Map each workflow from your [Workflow Audit](./workflow-audit-checklist.md) to a tier
2. Write the tier's checkpoint requirement into the actual workflow — this becomes the "process" pillar redesign
3. Revisit tier assignments periodically; a workflow's risk level can change (a routine social post becomes higher-risk during a live issue, for example)

**Note:** governance built as a design principle means the checkpoint is built into the workflow tool or process itself (e.g., a required approval step in the CMS) — not a policy document people are expected to remember to follow.
