# 4. Implementation Roadmap

A phased path, roughly 2-3 quarters end to end depending on organization size. Don't compress the sequence — the most common cause of stalled AI initiatives in comms is skipping straight to Phase 3.

## Phase 1 — Prove it narrow (Weeks 1–6)

- Complete the assessment (see [Assessment Methodology](./03-assessment-methodology.md))
- Pick ONE high-volume, low-risk workflow as the pilot — media monitoring digests, internal FAQ drafts, and social copy variants are common starting points
- Redesign that single workflow across all four pillars: define the role split, the process stage-gates, the tool, and the review policy
- Run it for 4-6 weeks with a simple before/after metric (time to publish, output volume, error rate)

The goal of Phase 1 is a credible internal case study, not org-wide transformation. This is what earns the mandate for Phase 2.

## Phase 2 — Build the shared infrastructure (Months 2–4)

- Take what worked in Phase 1 and generalize it: a shared prompt library, a documented review checklist, a standard for what "good AI-assisted output" looks like
- Expand to 3-5 more workflows, prioritized by the volume/risk grid
- Formalize the RACI for AI-assisted decisions (see [RACI Template](../toolkit/raci-template.md)) — this is where governance stops being informal
- Start tracking metrics that map to business outcomes the C-suite already cares about, not just internal efficiency metrics

## Phase 3 — Institutionalize (Months 4–6+)

- Governance becomes written policy, not team norm — risk tiers, sign-off requirements, and accountability are documented and apply function-wide
- Roles and career paths are formally updated to reflect the new skill mix
- The operating model is reviewed on a cadence (quarterly is reasonable) rather than treated as a one-time project
- The function can present a portfolio of redesigned workflows with attached metrics — this is the C-suite conversation BCG's research points to as the differentiator for AI leaders

## What "done" looks like

Not a fixed end state — an operating rhythm where new workflows get evaluated against the four-pillar framework by default, rather than AI adoption happening ad hoc, workflow by workflow, person by person.
