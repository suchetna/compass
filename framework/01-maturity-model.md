# 1. The Comms AI Maturity Model

Three stages, defined by operating behavior — not tool count.

## Stage 1 — Laggard (~70% of functions)

- AI used ad hoc, by individuals, for individual tasks (drafting, summarizing)
- No shared workflow, prompt library, or quality standard across the team
- No defined decision rights: unclear what AI output can ship without review
- Success measured anecdotally ("it saved me time") — no team-level metric
- AI treated as a personal productivity hack, not a functional capability

**Diagnostic question:** If your best AI user left tomorrow, would the function's AI usage disappear with them? If yes, you're here.

## Stage 2 — Emerging (~33% of functions)

- AI embedded in specific high-volume workflows (media monitoring, first-draft internal updates, social copy)
- Some shared standards exist — a prompt library, a review checklist — but inconsistently followed
- Decision rights exist for some workflows, not others
- Team-level metrics exist for the piloted workflows (time saved, output volume) but not tied to business outcomes
- One or two workflows have been fully redesigned around AI; the rest remain unchanged

**Diagnostic question:** Can you name the workflows that have been redesigned, and explain why those and not others? If yes, you're here.

## Stage 3 — Leader (~10% of functions)

- Operating model redesigned deliberately: roles, workflows, decision rights, and governance all updated together
- Clear, written policy on human-in-the-loop requirements by workflow type and risk level
- AI performance tied to business metrics the C-suite already tracks (share of voice, response time, engagement, crisis containment time)
- Governance is a design principle embedded in workflow, not a compliance layer checked after the fact
- The function can walk into the C-suite with evidence, not anecdotes

**Diagnostic question:** Could you show a board member a before/after of one redesigned workflow, with a number attached? If yes, you're here.

## Using this model

Most functions self-report higher than they operate. Be honest in the assessment — the toolkit's [AI Readiness Assessment](../toolkit/ai-readiness-assessment.md) is built to surface the gap between perceived and actual maturity, because that gap is usually where the real work starts.
